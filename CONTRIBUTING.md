## Participation Policy

This repository is available publicly.

Active participation (issues, pull requests, comments) is limited to students officially enrolled in the course and teaching staff.

External users are welcome to read, reuse, and learn from the material, but contributions will not be accepted.

## Ethical Conduct and Common Sense

This repository is part of an academic course and a collaborative learning environment. 
All participants are expected to act with professionalism, integrity, and respect.

- Disagreements are welcome when expressed respectfully and supported by reasoning or evidence.

### 2. Academic Integrity
- Do not submit work that is not your own without proper attribution.
- Always cite external sources, references, code snippets, or AI-assisted contributions when relevant.
- Do not misrepresent authorship or contributions.

### 3. Responsible Collaboration
- Keep discussions relevant to the technical topic.
- Avoid unnecessary noise or low-effort contributions.
- Do not intentionally disrupt workflows, discussions, or repository structure.

### 4. Data and Privacy Awareness
- Do not upload sensitive, confidential, or personal data.
- Do not include credentials, API keys, or private institutional information.
- Be mindful that this repository is publicly visible.

### 5. Common Sense Rule
If you are unsure whether something is appropriate to post or submit, pause and ask yourself:
- Is this respectful?
- Is this academically honest?
- Would I be comfortable defending this in a professional setting?

If the answer is uncertain, ask the instructor before proceeding.

---

Failure to follow these principles may result in removal of repository access.
